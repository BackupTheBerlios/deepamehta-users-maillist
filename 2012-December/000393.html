Dear DeepaMehta Users & Friends,

on behalf of the https://eduzen.tu-berlin.de-Team I am happy to announce
that we just released the DeepaMehta MathJax Renderer for DeepaMehta
users, compatible with DeepaMehta from today on (nightly builds for
testing purposes are available at http://demo.deepamehta.de) and most
probably also with the upcoming DeepaMehta 4.0.13 release.

Installation, configuration and usage hints are to find here:
https://github.com/mukil/dm4-mathjax-renderer

Download of the latest DeepaMehta MathJax Renderer Plugin is made
available here: https://github.com/mukil/dm4-mathjax-renderer/downloads

Live-Demo on Dr. Carol J.V. Fisher's math site (see MathJax in action)
http://www.onemathematicalcat.org/MathJaxDocumentation/TeXSyntax.htm

MathJax Project Website
http://www.mathjax.org/

We are happy for feedback and I am personally in the hope that the
installation description works as expected for you, resp. gives you
valueable insights on how to install and configure third party
extensions (like this custom renderer) for the DeepaMehta software
platform in general.

Cheers & Happy MathJaxxing!

Malte
